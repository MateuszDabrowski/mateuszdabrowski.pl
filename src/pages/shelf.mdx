### Frequently asked questions
    </div>

<details className={styles.faqDetails}>
  <summary>Where is my data stored?</summary>

  On your device, and synced automatically to your private iCloud account via Apple's CloudKit. Nothing is stored on any server I control. See the [privacy policy](#privacy-policy) below for full details.
</details>

<details className={styles.faqDetails}>
  <summary>How secure is my Stuff?</summary>

  Very. The labels (names) and values of your Stuff are encrypted locally on your device using AES-256-GCM before they are ever sent to iCloud. The encryption key is stored securely in your iCloud Keychain, meaning only your signed-in devices can read your data.
</details>

<details className={styles.faqDetails}>
  <summary>Can I search for my Stuff using macOS/iOS Spotlight?</summary>

  Yes, but it is turned off by default. If you enable Spotlight Search in Settings, your Stuff *names* (labels) will be stored unencrypted in your device's local search index so the OS can find them instantly. Your Stuff *values* remain fully encrypted and are never indexed.
</details>

<details className={styles.faqDetails}>
  <summary>Why isn't a folder I made on my Mac showing up on my iPhone?</summary>

  Shelf allows you to customize visibility per platform. Check the folder's settings to ensure "Visible on iOS" is toggled on. If it is, ensure both devices are signed into the same iCloud account and check the Sync Status in Shelf's Settings menu.
</details>

## Privacy Policy {#privacy-policy}

<p style={{fontStyle: 'italic', color: 'var(--ifm-color-emphasis-600)'}}>Last updated: May 6, 2026</p>

<p>Shelf is built by Mateusz Dąbrowski. This policy explains what data the Shelf app handles and how. It is short because Shelf is built from the ground up for privacy.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>
### Data Shelf stores
    </div>

<p>When you use Shelf, your data is saved to your device and synced to **your private iCloud account** through Apple's CloudKit service. Specifically:</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Stuff Labels and Values:</strong> Fully end-to-end encrypted (AES-256-GCM) on your device before syncing.</li>
  <li><strong>Folder Names and Stuff Types:</strong> Synced securely via iCloud but are not locally encrypted.</li>
  <li><strong>Preferences:</strong> Your sort order, platform visibility toggles, and other app settings.</li>
</ul>

<p>This data never reaches a server controlled by me. It is stored in your iCloud private database and is visible only to you, on devices signed into the same Apple ID. I — the developer — cannot see it.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>
### Data Shelf does not collect
    </div>

<p>Shelf does **not** collect, transmit, or store any of the following:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Personal information (name, address, phone, email).</li>
  <li>Account credentials.</li>
  <li>Analytics, telemetry, crash reports, or usage data.</li>
  <li>Advertising identifiers.</li>
  <li>Location data.</li>
</ul>

<p>There are no third-party SDKs in the app and no external servers the app talks to.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>
### Clipboard Access
    </div>

<p>Shelf only reads text from your clipboard when you explicitly paste data into the Add/Edit form. The app's auto-detection features (guessing if text is a URL, email, etc.) run strictly on the text you are actively editing. Shelf **never** monitors or reads your system clipboard in the background.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>
### Spotlight Search
    </div>

<p>By default, Shelf does not share any data with the system search. If you explicitly enable Spotlight Search in the app's Security settings, the names (labels) of your Stuff will be stored unencrypted in your device's local system index so you can search for them. The actual contents (values) of your Stuff remain encrypted and are never shared with Spotlight.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>
### Security & iCloud
    </div>

<p>The master encryption key used to secure your Stuff is generated locally and stored securely in your iCloud Keychain. Apple's privacy policy governs how iCloud handles your data: <a href="https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/">apple.com/legal/privacy</a>. If you sign out of iCloud, the app continues to work — your library is stored locally on the device, but changes will stop syncing to other devices.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>
### Changes to this policy
    </div>

<p>If the policy changes, I'll update the "Last updated" date above and post the new version at the same URL. Material changes will be called out in the app's release notes.</p>

### Contact
