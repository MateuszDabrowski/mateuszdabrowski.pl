### Frequently asked questions

    </div>

<details className={styles.faqDetails}>
  <summary>Where are my songs stored?</summary>

  On your device, and synced to your private iCloud account. Nothing is stored on a server I control. See the [privacy policy](#privacy-policy) below for details.

</details>

<details className={styles.faqDetails}>
  <summary>A song I made on iPad isn't showing up on iPhone.</summary>

  Both devices need to be signed into the same iCloud account, with iCloud Drive on. Open Strum's **Settings → About** tab — if it says "Local-only fallback", iCloud isn't reachable; sign into iCloud in the system Settings app and re-launch Strum. Sync usually catches up within 30 seconds.

</details>

<details className={styles.faqDetails}>
  <summary>I deleted a song by accident.</summary>

  Songs go to **Trash** for 7 days before they're permanently removed. Open the Trash section in the sidebar, swipe the song, and tap **Restore**.

</details>

<details className={styles.faqDetails}>
  <summary>The tuner says my strings are wildly out of tune.</summary>

  Make sure you've selected the right tuning in **Song header → Tuning** (high-G, low-G, or guitar standard). The tuner expects the notes for the active tuning.

</details>

<details className={styles.faqDetails}>
  <summary>Can I print or share a song as PDF?</summary>

  Yes — open the song, tap the share icon, and choose to export as a **PDF**, an **Image**, or a native **.strum** file (perfect for sending to other Strum users). When exporting a PDF or Image, the output will exactly match what is currently configured in your view—if you toggle off the staff or lyrics in the app, they will be hidden in the export too. To export many songs at once, use **Settings → Export library**.

</details>

<details className={styles.faqDetails}>
  <summary>Does Strum support [feature X]?</summary>

  The current scope is described above. If the feature you need isn't there, write in — I read every email and feature requests inform what ships next.

</details>

## Privacy Policy {#privacy-policy}

<p style={{fontStyle: 'italic', color: 'var(--ifm-color-emphasis-600)'}}>Last updated: May 4, 2026</p>

<p>Strum is built by Mateusz Dąbrowski. This policy explains what data the Strum app handles and how. It is short because Strum collects very little.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>

### Data Strum stores

    </div>

<p>When you create a song, folder, chord shape, strum pattern, or preference inside Strum, that data is saved to your device and synced to **your private iCloud account** through Apple's CloudKit service. Specifically:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Songs and their sections, measures, beats, tab notes, and lyrics.</li>
  <li>Folders and the songs contained in them.</li>
  <li>Custom chord shapes you create.</li>
  <li>Custom strum patterns you create.</li>
  <li>App preferences (default tempo, tuning, time signature, default fingerings, metronome settings, follow-tab options).</li>
</ul>

<p>This data never reaches a server controlled by me. It is stored in your iCloud private database and is visible only to you, on devices signed into the same Apple ID. I — the developer — cannot see it.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>

### Data Strum does not collect

    </div>

<p>Strum does **not** collect, transmit, or store any of the following:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Personal information (name, address, phone, email).</li>
  <li>Account credentials.</li>
  <li>Analytics, telemetry, crash reports, or usage data.</li>
  <li>Advertising identifiers.</li>
  <li>Location data.</li>
  <li>Contacts, photos, calendars, or other system data.</li>
</ul>

<p>There are no third-party SDKs in the app and no external servers the app talks to.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>

### Microphone

    </div>

<p>Strum requests microphone permission only when you open the **in-app tuner**. The tuner listens to a string you pluck and tells you whether it's in tune. Audio is processed entirely on-device, in real time, and is **never recorded, saved, or transmitted**. If you deny microphone permission, every other part of Strum continues to work normally — only the tuner is affected.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>

### iCloud

    </div>

<p>Strum's sync uses your iCloud account. Apple's privacy policy governs how iCloud handles your data: [apple.com/legal/privacy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/). If you sign out of iCloud, or disable iCloud for Strum in Settings, the app continues to work — your library is stored locally on the device. New changes simply stop syncing to other devices.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>

### Children's privacy

    </div>

<p>Strum is rated 4+ and is suitable for all ages. Because Strum does not collect any personal information, it complies with COPPA and similar laws by design.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>

### Deleting your data

    </div>

<p>Songs you delete in Strum go to a **Trash** folder for 7 days, then are permanently removed from your iCloud private database. You can also empty the Trash manually from the sidebar.</p>

<p>To remove all Strum data from iCloud at once: delete the app from each of your devices, then go to **Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage → Strum** on iOS, or **System Settings → Apple Account → iCloud → Manage → Strum** on macOS, and tap **Delete Data**.</p>

    <div style={{marginTop: '2rem'}}>

### Changes to this policy

    </div>

<p>If the policy changes, I'll update the "Last updated" date above and post the new version at the same URL. Material changes will be called out in the app's release notes.</p>
